Having a solid foundation in mathematics, I am equipped with a comprehensive understanding of statistics and probability theory. My enthusiasm for programming has propelled me to expand my expertise and knowledge, leading me to pursue a Master's Degree in a Data Science-related program. There I engaged in several projects centered around the implementation of the Python programming language, primarily focusing on topics within the realm of data science.

My passion about data science began with a curiosity about the power of data to drive innovation and solve complex problems. Initially, I found myself drawn by beautiful data visualizations and the ability to uncover hidden patterns and trends in large datasets. As I delved further into these captivating realms, I uncovered the profound impact that statistics could have when translated into algorithms and executed on a computer.

Witnessing how these algorithms could streamline processes and simplify complexities was profoundly inspiring. I found myself drawn to the inherent beauty of implementing machine learning models, each line of code representing a step towards unraveling new insights and creating tangible solutions. Throughout this journey, I have come to appreciate the meticulous process involved in crafting meaningful outcomes from raw data. From the seemingly mundane task of preprocessing datasets to the meticulous process of fine-tuning models, each stage of the data science journey can and shall be viewed as a source of accomplishment! These incremental achievements culminate in the fulfillment and joy that comes rushing in when a project is finished! For me, therefore, data science is a place where creativity intertwines with analytical rigor, a means to make art through the analytical lens of science!

Brief Outline of Projects:


  • Analysis Project on 2 Datasets offered by the Frail Safe Project:


    The goal of this project can be split into 3 discrete parts:
    Preprocessing of the clinical dataset and the implementation of multiple classification models on the labeled data. Preprocessing of the beacons dataset, mergence of the two processed datasets and implementation of multiple clustering algorithms for the data. Exploratory analysis with various visualization techniques & evaluation of all the classification and clustering models that were used.


  • Spark NLP Project for Fake Job Postings Detection:


    This project revolves around a classification problem, aiming to the development of a model capable of differentiating fake job postings from real ones. In order to achieve this goal, the big data processing engine Apache Spark was used on top of python implementation language. The main PySpark library used was Spark NLP. The models developed are required to use only the description column from the job postings csv file for training.


  • Queries using PySpark on Recycled & Waste Collection Data, offered by Open Data Buffalo:


    The data offered contain the information of the amount and type of waste collected for recycling in the city of Buffalo at the state of New York. The goal was to implement a program in the Apache Spark environment, which will integrate these data, and then answer the following combined queries: What was the average amount of waste for each material type? For how many years was the total amount of Scrap Metal (or of a material of our choice) higher than 5 other materials (of our choice). Which are those years? What are the 5 materials with the largest amounts of recycling per year? For which year each material had the minimum amount of recycling and which its maximum? Which are the 5 months with the largest amount of recyclable items? (regardless of year and type) Which are the total quantities of recyclable materials per year? How much recycled ammount did each material have through these years?


  • Analysis of home appliance energy use and machine learning approaches to the energy disaggregation problem:


    This project was part of my master thesis, where the primary objective was to implement and evaluate various machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL) models for regression and forecasting tasks. The focus was on utilizing a house's aggregated signal to predict and forecast consumption-related variables, such as the active and reactive power of individual appliances. In order to access the whole thesis, you can visit the Nemertes Library webpage. Currently the thesis is available only in Greek Language, however it is highly probable that a part of this work will be submitted as a Paper in the AlgoCloud 2024 Conference.
